## Gameplay

### How Additions work

In the game, Additions are explained by **Master Tasman in Seles** who can give the player the full tutorial of how additions work.

When the player chooses the "Attack" option from the command menu and picks a target, the character will charge in and perform an initial strike at the enemy. While doing so, **two blue squares** will pop up, one fixed in the center and the other on the outside edges of the screen, zooming into the central square. When the outside square aligns with the center square, the player should **press the X button**. If timed correctly the combined square will flash **white** and the addition will continue. If the player misses the timing, the center of the square will turn **Blue** if too slow or **Gray** if too fast.

**The number of correctly timed inputs required in an addition is the number of strikes minus one.** For example, Dart's initial addition Double Slash is a two-hit combo and requires one correct input.

During the longer additions, there is chance for the enemy to **counterattack**. When it does, the squares will turn **red** with a sound. Instead of X, the player must hit the **circle button**. If timed correctly, breaks the counter and continues the addition. If missed, addition ends and the character takes damage.

### Addition Levels

Each character initially starts with **one addition** (always a two-hit combo). They unlock new additions at set character levels, **except each person's final addition which is unlocked by mastering all other additions**.

Once an addition has been completed, its **use counter** will increase. When it has been used and completed **20 times**, it will level up and improve. For some additions, both damage multiplier and SP gain may go up, for some only one of them. Once the player has successfully performed the addition **80 times**, it reaches its **maximum level and highest efficiency**.

### Items supporting Additions

Two accessories that make additions complete (done without needing to press a button) :

- **Wargod Calling** : completes each addition but does **half damage**, gives **half SP** and **does not add to the use counter** (no Addition Level progression). **Price : 1,000 G** at Lohan or Fletz.
- **Ultimate Wargod** : completes additions with **full damage**, **full SP** and **adds to the use counter**. **Price : 10,000 G** at Lohan, OR rare drop at Phantom Ship (save state abuse possible). Note critique fandom : blocks Accessory Spot for "basically no inherent advantage apart from easier or more relaxed battles".

### Player Guidance

_Section partly subjective._ Key learnings :

- **Mastering additions = crucial** for success in TLoD canon, particularly boss battles
- **Visual cue (square zooming in) > sound cue / rhythm memorization** :
  - Visual reliance scales to new additions easily
  - Rhythm memorization breaks on counterattacks
  - Some additions have too long intervals to memorize (e.g. Kongol's Inferno, Meru's Perky Step)
  - Sound cues confused by character battle cries + BGM
- **Initial / early additions** (2-3 hits highest level) often outdamage late multi-hit additions if those are botched
- Initial addition of each character : at least **150% damage multiplier** + at least **34 SP** at highest level

#### Additions exempt from counterattacks (fandom list, more explicit than wiki LoD)

- Enemies **cannot counter the first or last input** → additions with 1-2 inputs never counterattacked
- **All Kongol's Additions** (none counterable)
- **Madness Hero** (Dart)
- **Blazing Dynamo** (Dart)
- **5-Ring Shattering** (Haschel)
- **Omni-Sweep** (Haschel)
- **Hammer Spin** (Meru)

## Dragoon Additions

Whenever a character transforms into a Dragoon, they can use the **Dragoon Addition** as their regular attack. Mechanic :

- A **light** rotates **clockwise in a circle similar to a clock**
- Player presses **X** when the light is **at the top**
- **Speed of rotating increases with each successful input** (next press becomes more difficult)
- **Maximum of 4 correct inputs** to extend the combo

> ⚠️ **Divergence vs Wulves doc** : Wulves dit max **5 Successful Inputs** (Kongol limité à 4). Fandom dit max **4** general. À résoudre via tier 1 confirmation.

## Reception

Mixed reception. IGN (David Smith) et RPGFan (Neal Chandran) ont critiqué la précision exigée et la lenteur du combat. Notable mention de la comparaison "playing Parappa during every battle" (IGN). Communauté fan reste positive — additions = élément défini du jeu.

## Trivia

- **Kongol scripted counter** : During the battle with Kongol at **Hoax** and at the **Black Castle**, if the player misses an addition, Kongol performs a special "counter attack" damaging the character. Also happens at the **first stage of the final boss fight**.
- **"Flurry of Styx" est une typo** — le vrai nom canon est **"Ferry of Styx"** (référence canon japonaise "Sanzu no Wata" 三途の渡 = "Ferry of the River Sanzu"). Le texte final du combo affiche le nom correct ; Haschel dit le nom erroné vocalement.
- **Haschel's additions** = toutes nommées d'après le nombre de hits (référence numérologique japonaise — Ferry of Styx ≈ River of three worlds, kanji 三 = trois).
- **Meru's Final Addition (Perky Step)** = **plus haut damage rate du jeu à 600%**. Meru peut aussi gagner **le plus de SP** : Pretty Hammer + Wargod's Sash + Cool Boogie mastered = **495 SP** d'une seule addition.
- **Lavitz learns Additions the fastest** : Lv5 Spinning Cane, Lv7 Rod Typhoon, Lv11 Gust of Wind Dance. Joins party at Lv3.
- **Meru learns Additions the slowest** : Cat's Cradle at Lv30.
- **"Flower Storm" → voice line variation** : Albert vocal = **"Blossom Storm"** ; Lavitz vocal = **"Rose Storm"**. Texte affiché = même (le nom canon affiché varie selon la source : wiki LoD = "Flower Storm", fandom = "Blossom Storm" pour Albert section). Version japonaise unifie : both say **"Cherry Blossom Blizzard"** (桜の吹雪, _sakura fubuki_).

Experience points (EXP) are awarded upon defeating Enemies in battle. Each party member has a threshold of experience needed before they can level up. Leveling up permanently increases a character's **maximum HP, attack, defense, magic attack, magic defense**, and amount of experience needed to get to the next level. This remains true until a character reaches **level 60**, after which they can no longer level up.

## Distribution

**Active party members who survived the encounter**, meaning they did not end the encounter with 0 HP or while afflicted with Petrification, **split the awarded EXP between one another**. Any EXP that does not divide evenly is lost. For example if an encounter awards 100 EXP and three party members survived, then each gets 33 and the remaining point is lost. If two party members are alive the EXP is split in half so each gains 50, while a sole survivor collects all 100 EXP for themselves. **Those who did not survive gain 0 EXP**.

**Inactive party members receive 50% (rounded down) of what any active member received**. Keeping with the previous example, if all three party members survive they each get 33 EXP and then each inactive member would receive 16. With two surviving members getting 50, inactive ones would get 25. A single survivor getting 100 would mean every inactive member would collect 50 EXP. **As a result, with enough inactive party members the group as a whole receives more experience with only a single survivor than if all three active members had lived.**

### Total party EXP multiplier (vs post-battle display)

|                 | Inactive 1 | Inactive 2 | Inactive 3 | Inactive 4 |
| --------------- | ---------- | ---------- | ---------- | ---------- |
| **Survivors 1** | 1.5×       | 2×         | 2.5×       | 3×         |
| **Survivors 2** | 1.25×      | 1.5×       | 1.75×      | 2×         |
| **Survivors 3** | ~1.16×     | ~1.33×     | 1.5×       | ~1.66×     |

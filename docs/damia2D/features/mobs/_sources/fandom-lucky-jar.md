## Appearance

This monster appears as a **physically large gold-colored jar with a smiling face engraved into it**. It has a **large nose, two wide open eyes and gleaming teeth**. The **handles resembles ears and the cover looks like a hat**.

---

## Battle

**Lucky Jar cannot be hurt by Physical or Magical Attacks, but instead can only be hurt by Poison**. The **player should preferably use Poison Needles which can be bought at Ulara for 20 Gold**. If these are **unavailable, it is possible to use Miranda and equip her with the Virulent Arrow which can inflict Poison upon a target with a given probability**. In this case, it is **recommended to use the Magical Stone of Signet and Speed Down to prevent it from running away and to poison it, as well as Speed Up on Miranda**. If the **Lucky Jar is poisoned, it takes 1 damage per turn and 6 turns are required to defeat it**.

**Sachets will instantly eliminate this monster as these will always deal 10 damage**, however, they are **very limited and difficult to obtain by farming these in the Home of Gigantos**.

The Lucky Jar uses the following attacks:

- **Attack** — It **rushes in and deals 10% of the character's max HP as damage**.
- **Panic Bell** — Inflicts **Confusion upon a single target**.

---

## Drops

Upon defeat, the Lucky Jar is **guaranteed to drop the item Moon Serenade, an item which cannot be bought in shops and restores all MP for all characters in the party**.

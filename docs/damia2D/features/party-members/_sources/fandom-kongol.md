**Kongol** (コンゴール, _Kongōru_) is the **last surviving individual of the Gigantos, the 97th species from the Divine Tree**.

---

## Character Design

Kongol is a **tall and muscular Giganto with black hair in the style of a mohawk, red markings over each of his eyes that resembles face paint or scars, and eyes with apparently no irises**.

As an initial boss character, and when he joins the party later, he is seen **shirtless and wears only a brown cross belted armor with a skull insignia at the center of his chest, yellow pauldrons in each side of his shoulder, a brown loincloth with a black belt with a bull shaped buckle and traditional black furred with steel strapped gray boots**. He also wears **traditional brown gloves with black furred sleeves and wields a giant axe**.

When he faces Dart and the group before they confront Emperor Doel, Kongol is seen in **traditional Giganto armor similar to his brother Indora, but with a skull motif, and he fights using claws instead of his giant axe**. The color of his armor is **light gold and black**. However, when he was defeated, his **armor is destroyed to reveal his primary outfit, which was concealed under his traditional armor**.

As a Dragoon, **Kongol bears a strong resemblance to Belzac's Dragoon form**.

He wears **gold Dragoon heavy armor with indigo blue accents and a green gem at the center of his armor, a matching gold greaves which is connected to his armor, indigo blue pants, gold knee-high Dragoon armor boots with indigo blue round designs in the center and his giant axe transformed into a large Dragoon Axe in a steel motif**. He also wears a **matching gold gauntlets, a gold v-shaped dragon headband and his Dragoon wings are light orange, light yellow and gold with black metallic outlines**.

While in this form, the accents of his Dragoon armor are **capri blue and his Dragoon wings appear as light purple, light green and gold with black metallic outlines**.

## Personality

Kongol is **extremely loyal to someone that cares about him and will do anything to protect his allies**. His loyalty is seen both towards **Emperor Doel and Dart**, to which Kongol seeks to repay their kindness. He is also **serious and fierce in the battlefield**. His **kind, straightforward and honest personality makes him easily accepted by the group**, although his **closest teammate is most likely Dart**.

As a Giganto warrior, **Kongol is not particularly educated and cannot read human letters**, however, he is shown to have a **strong intuition, an innate sense of danger as well as stronger five senses compared to his fellow Humans or Winglies**. This is exemplified by him **recognizing the smell of blood in the ancient colosseum in the Forbidden Land**, recognizing a **picture symbol in a teleporter room as a Giganto symbol at the Forbidden Land** and **sensing the danger in the Kashua Glacier when the group is about to face Windigo**.

## Gameplay

### Abilities and Attributes

Kongol is a **very polarizing character, with an enormous HP pool as well as the strongest physical attributes in the entire group, while at the same time having the worst magical abilities and slowest speed**. His physical strength is notorious for giving him massive attacking power even with his starting addition, as well as the ability to barely take a scratch from physical attacks by the majority of enemies.

These strengths, however, come at the pricy cost of him being **extremely susceptible to magical attacks**, particularly in boss fights, where he tends to lose the majority (if not outright all) of his HP when hit with virtually any stronger magic spell. Another glaring weakness of Kongol is his **terrible speed, the lowest of the entire cast at 30**.

Kongol's Dragoon form is **rather unimpressive as well, with his Dragoon Magic being extremely weak due to the aforementioned lack of magical attack despite a base 200% stat boost, having only three Dragoon Magic spells instead of the usual four**, and being the **only character who has not a 5-count, but a 4-count Dragoon addition**, which is easier to pull off, but presumably weaker.

It is also beneficial that he has the **lowest amount of additions among the cast**, as this allows him to **quickly progress to his final addition, Bone Crusher**. While it only has a **maxed damage multiplier of 300%, the lowest among all final additions**, Kongol's tremendous base attacking power makes up for it.

### Dragoon

#### Acquiring the Dragoon Spirit

There are **two different ways to obtain Kongol's Dragoon Spirit**. The first way is to **buy it for 1,000 Gold from the merchant in Lohan who gave the player the bottle during the events of Disc 1**. If the player is planning on using Kongol as a party member, this is **definitely the way to go**, as the only other way to acquire it is **nearly at the end of the game, when the player fights Kongol's brother, Indora, and also acquires Indora's Axe**. Getting it in Lohan **gives the player a lot more time to gain Dragoon Levels with Kongol**.

#### Dragoon Transformation

To transform into a Dragoon, **Kongol's Golden Dragoon Spirit glows in a white light and makes a powerful downward punch into the ground forming a massive fissure that also covers him in a large rock and is burst into pieces to reveal his Dragoon form**.

#### Dragoon Magic

Despite a high **200% base boost on magical attack**, Kongol's Dragoon magic is unimpressive, as he still lacks magical attack and has **low-damaging spells against all enemies but with no further effect**. It is arguably a **better strategy to rely on his Dragoon Addition, which is only 4-count instead of 5-count as with other characters, but deals significant damage nonetheless**.

During the battle at Hoax, **Kongol will take two "stances", one with his axe and one without**. When he has his axe out he has only **one basic attack with his axe**. When he switches to fighting with his bare hands, the fight becomes much harder, particularly if the player isn't consistent with additions. In this stance, Kongol has three attacks:

- **Lariat**: Kongol runs at Dart or Lavitz, smashing his arm into their head.
- **Head Slam**: Kongol grabs both Dart and Lavitz and smashes their heads together, then throws them up in the air and bashes them against the wall.
- **Counter**: If the player miss one attack during the addition, Kongol will immediately stop the addition and begin wailling on the character before tossing them back.

The player has to **fight only with Dart and Lavitz**. **Using magical attack items (recommended with Dart due to his higher magical attack) can make this fight significantly easier**, as Kongol takes vast amounts of damage when hit with magic.

The player had to **face a Sandora Elite right before the battle with Kongol**.

As usual at the end of a boss fight, a cutscene begins and **Dart rushes forward to deliver the finishing blow. However, Kongol raises his forearm and strikes Dart to the ground. However, Dart is saved by his Dragoon powers that are awakened by Rose**.

## Story

### Before the events of the game

Kongol is the **sole known survivor of the Gigantos, the 97th race from the Divine Tree**. He once had an **older brother, Indora, who was a very powerful Giganto, the most powerful as claimed by Kongol**. The rest of his kind was **slain by humans for fearing their size and power**.

However, about **20 years ago, he was saved by Doel, the brother of the King of Serdio**. Under Doel's leadership and his **ideals for a world where all species are equal, Kongol became a tough and strong warrior fighting for Imperial Sandora**.

### Chapter 1: The Serdian War

#### Hoax

When Dart, Lavitz, and Shana arrive at Hoax, they assist in defending the town against Imperial Sandora. During the nightly raid, Dart and Lavitz are challenged by the Giganto warrior. After a hard fight, **Dart rushes in to deliver a finishing blow, but Kongol knocks him to the ground**. As Kongol raises his axe to finish off Dart, suddenly, there is a glimpse light appears in the sky, as **Rose, a Dragoon warrior comes in and interrupts the fight. She calls upon the Dragoon Spirit of Dart**, and Dart begins to glow. As Dart transforms into a Dragoon, a burst of energy flings Kongol away. **With Kongol knocked out, a Sandoran Soldier calls for retreat, with Kongol vowing to return**.

#### Black Castle

Kongol appears one more time in the Black Castle in Kazas, when Dart and his companions approach Emperor Doel's throne room. In a **powerful Giganto armor**, he faces off against the group again, but is eventually defeated. **Rose destroys his armor, and as Kongol falls to his knees, he begs them to finish him off, as he sees himself unable to live with the shame of having lost despite of his strong armor**.

**Rose is eager to fulfill his wish, but is stopped by Shana**. Kongol stays down on his knees, **shivering, and wondering why Dart and his group are kind to him despite being enemies**.

### Chapter 2: Platinum Shadow

#### Home of Gigantos

Dart and his group chase the Gehrich Gang to their headquarters at the ancient Home of Gigantos. They defeat Gehrich and Mappi, but **Mappi activates a trap causing a giant stone pillar to fall down onto the group. Kongol rushes in and stops it from crushing the group, using his herculean strength**.

Kongol reveals that he is the **sole survivor of the Gigantos, and on his intention to join Dart**.

> "**You enemy no more. Here Holy place of Gigantos. Me survivor. Human killed us. They stole Giganto's treasure. Human thinks other species can die. Human selfish creature. [...] It's for Emperor Doel. Me still small and ran. He saved Kongol. He fought against bad people. Kongol comes with you. So Kongol is here. Emperor Doel say he make world where every species equal. World needs strong leader. Whether you can be leader, Kongol will watch to the end. Giganto's sadness should not be repeated.... [...] You defeat Emperor Doel. Strong strong power. Stronger than Emperor Doel. Kongol wants to see where the power goes.**"
>
> — **Kongol**

Dart gladly accept this and says that **Kongol, Dart and the group are now friends**.

> "**...Friend. It sounds good.**"
>
> — **Kongol**

#### Queen Fury

On board of the Queen Fury, **Haschel finds Kongol alone in the ship's furnace room**. As Kongol states that he is always alone, **Haschel assures him that he isn't alone anymore**, even after losing Emperor Doel, and recommends Kongol to **speak to Doel's nephew Albert**.

Kongol asks Albert for the **meaning of friends reflecting on Dart's words**.

> "**Maybe we can put it this way. Maybe 'friends' are the people who walk on the same road. People who walk together, but not led by somebody, who share happiness and pain. Yes. That is us now. Although we have different objectives, we are walking side by side. Kongol. You are already walking the same road as us.**"
>
> — **Albert to Kongol**

#### Fueno

> "**You, die, impossible.**"
>
> — **Kongol** (on Dart and Rose's survival)

#### Fourth Visit to Fletz

At the banquet, where Dart searches for Shana, Kongol tells Dart that he **cannot see her despite his height**.

### Chapter 3: Fate & Soul

#### Deningrad

Kongol finds a book about Winglies, but admits that he **cannot read human letters**.

> "**It is about Winglies, I guess. Kongol cannot read human characters. Kongol looks pictures.**"

#### Forest of Winglies

In the Forest of Winglies, **Ancestor Blano greets the group explicitly with "Humans and Giganto", recognizing Kongol as a guest from another race**.

#### Forbidden Land

**Kongol notices the smell of blood upon entering the space** (Colosseum). Later, when the group finds a room with symbols of different species born from the Divine Tree, **Kongol is the first to recognize that the symbol furthest to the left looks similar to Gigantos**.

#### Kashua Glacier

**Kongol senses the danger and warns the group to prepare for battle** vs Windigo.

> "**Kongol feels. Monster angry. About we entered here. [...] Monster, don't know coldness. Monster only knows to kill. It wants kill us.**"
>
> — **Kongol**

### Chapter 4: Moon & Fate

#### Ulara

In Ulara, Kongol appears to have **abandoned the idea about a "leader"**, but vows to go with Dart anyway.

> "**We don't know what will happen in ancient city of Winglies. We need strong weapon. Kongol doesn't care about leader of creatures. Kongol goes with you to the end.**"
>
> — **Kongol**

#### Aglis

In the Magical City Aglis, **Kongol undergoes a test of courage by Savan**. He first sees a **vision of Rose who challenges him to a practice fight and verbally provokes him, mentioning his losses before and his "cheap pride of a Giganto"**. Kongol, however, stays calm and **realizes that the vision he sees is not Rose**.

> "**Kongol and Rose changed. Rose doesn't say such things. You are not Rose.**"
>
> — **Kongol**

Then, **Emperor Doel appears behind Kongol, draws his sword and presses Kongol on allying with his former enemies**.

> "**Don't tell me you are buddying up with your enemy. You fool! What happened to my plan, the unification of Endiness!? Have you found the leader who replaced me!?**"
>
> — **Emperor Doel**

Kongol, however, stays calm again, **does not swing the axe and instead takes Doel's sword away, making the vision of Doel disappear**.

> "**Emperor Doel, Kongol got new feeling. Kongol found new friends. Intention of Emperor Doel is in Kongol. Kongol won't avoid Emperor Doel.**"
>
> — **Kongol**

> "**Real courage can be obtained when a person is released from the bonds of the past.**"
>
> — **Savan**

#### Zenebatos

In Zenebatos, as Dart and his companions revise laws of the Winglies to proceed to the Signet Sphere, they are **teleported into a jail by guardian Laptos**. The jail appears to be a room with no exit, and as the group finds no way out, **they notice that Kongol is missing**. Just at this time, **Kongol destroys the walls of the jail and frees his companions, as he was too heavy to be teleported into the jail**.

#### Moon That Never Sets

In the Moon That Never Sets, Kongol is separated from Dart and Rose alongside Albert, Meru, Haschel and Miranda. He **confronts his own past, as he witnesses the scene when he was originally saved by Emperor Doel in the ancient Home of Gigantos**. Across a massive cliff, **Bandits are raiding the Home of Gigantos, but are chased away by Doel and some of his soldiers**. They notice someone is hiding in a cavern, who actually is the **little kid Kongol**. Scared at first, he tries to send the humans away, but **Doel tells him of his visions for peace and equality of races and tells Kongol to go with him**. The young Kongol reveals that **everyone else except for him is dead now**.

Kongol then **descends alone to the room where Dart and his group previously fought Gehrich and Mappi to meet his brother Indora**.

**Kongol tells Indora that he became stronger and wants to overcome his brother**, who used to be the strongest of all Gigantos, to obtain the power to help Dart. The two **fight in a fierce duel, with Kongol emerging victorious**. Indora, having been defeated by his brother, acknowledges Kongol's strength.

> "**Kongol.... a hero. The strongest hero of Gigantos. Go, to the people who need your power. To fulfill desire is the power of destruction. To care for others is the power of truth. You.... received the power to overcome Indora.... The strongest power.... Kongol, the pride of Indora.... Well done....**"
>
> — **Indora**

In case Kongol did not receive his Dragoon Spirit at Lohan, **Indora hands him the Dragoon Spirit of the Gold Dragon**. From there, Kongol **returns to the party with his newfound power and as the new Hero of the Gigantos**.

> "**Emperor Doel would often say, 'To make a world where all species are equal, a strong leader is needed'. But Kongol knows. Anybody can make a world where all species are equal. Even when you are not Dragoon. Gigantos, Humans, Minintos, Winglies, anybody. Anybody having the feeling can do. But Kongol found out. Anybody can destroy the world too. Gigantos, Humans, Winglies, Gods, anybody. Kongol found out one more thing. We can stop him. Dragoons can save the world. Kongol, hero of Gigantos. Kongol doesn't fear god.**"
>
> — **Kongol**

#### Epilogue

As the group overcomes Melbu Frahma in the body of the God of Destruction, the Dragoons flee the Moon after Rose and Zieg make one last sacrificial charge. **Kongol is seen carrying Meru, and destroying obstacles on their way out**.

In the **epilogue FMV of the video, Kongol appears to have come to Rouge together with Haschel and practices the Rouge Style of Martial Arts**. As Haschel demonstrates a combination with a one-legged backflip, **Kongol falls down into the water when trying to copy it**.

## Trivia

- According to **The Legend of Dragoon Official Guidebook**, Kongol is **37 years old and 250cm (8'2") tall**.
- According to the Guidebook, the **armor he wears in the second boss fight is made of Dragon bones and gives magical power to the wearer**.
- Kongol's **idle pose is similar Rose's idle pose**; however, instead of crossing his arms over his chest, he **folds them like a pharaoh in a sarcophagus**.
- When defending, **Kongol holds his axe in front of him, the blade mainly covering his face**.
- When in critical health, Kongol **leans forward while slightly slouching and holds his stomach with his left arm and hand**.
- When dispirited, Kongol **slouches forward and slightly to the left with his knees slightly bent**.
- **Kongol is the only character able to have two ways to obtain his Dragoon Spirit**.
- When Kongol joins the party, the player can to Donau and **talk to Mr. Pelpee in the bar to get a hint where to find his Dragoon spirit**.
- **Kongol's Dragoon spirit is the only that is available for purchase**.
- If the player gets the Dragoon Spirit for Kongol by buying it, **he is the only one to be recognized by a Dragoon Spirit without having killed the previous owner or inheriting it**.
- The **armor Kongol wears in his 2nd boss encounter resembles the armor worn by Indora**.
- In the final FMV where the Dragoons escape from the Moon, **he carries Meru and destroys a rock that blocks their flight**.

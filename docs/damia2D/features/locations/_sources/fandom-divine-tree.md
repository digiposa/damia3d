The **Divine Tree is the Tree of Life**. All life comes from this Divine Tree.

The Divine Tree, sowed by the Creator Soa, is the **legendary progenitor of all life in Endiness**.

## History

> WARNING: This section contains major spoilers.

> _Fruit — A fruit of the Divine Tree._

The Divine Tree is the legend commonly accepted as the **story of creation** in the continent of Endiness. **Planted by the creator Soa on the barren earth**, it is believed to be the origin of all life. When its **108 fruits** ripened and fell to the ground, each gave rise to a species. Throughout the history of Endiness, **it existed only in legends and may never have been seen by a living being**. It is depicted as a **large tree with seven rooted branches**.

The intelligent species named in the game that arose from the fruits of the Divine Tree are :

- **Gigantos** (97th fruit)
- **Minintos** (99th fruit)
- **Dragons** (105th fruit)
- **Humans** (106th fruit)
- **Winglies** (107th fruit)
- **Virage and its subspecies** (108th fruit)

### Chapter 4: Moon & Fate

> _19-divinetree-033 — A bright flash originates from the Moon That Never Sets..._

After the **final Signet Sphere is destroyed at Mayfil**, the **Moon That Never Sets falls down to the earth**. In a **bright flash originating from the Moon**, the **Divine Tree reveals itself in a wide, vast desert-like land** somewhere on an unknown location on the continent of Endiness.

> _20-divinetree-035 — ...and subsequently reveals the legendary Divine Tree._

The **Moon drops onto the center of the Divine Tree**, **crushing the majority of its branches** and making them crumble, only leaving the **center and the bases of the original seven branches behind**. It then **links itself to the moon, with thin branches growing into it**, while **hundreds of Virage crawl out of the Moon, presumably as forebearers of the God of Destruction** that is supposed to be born from the **108th and final fruit** of the Divine Tree.

Dart and his group **ride on the back of Coolon** to get to the Moon, but **suffer a crash landing** after being **intercepted by a Virage, with Coolon dying in this process**. They subsequently work their way upwards to the Moon. On the Divine Tree, they encounter **many relics from the past, such as dried up fruits**, and use the **energy streams connecting the Divine Tree with the Moon** to work their way upwards. On their way, they **battle a new species from an unhatched fruit**, and eventually arrive at the Moon.

## Gameplay

The Divine Tree is a **dungeon found late in the game on CD 4** and the **introduction to the finale of the game** ; many of the **mysteries and legends of the game are resolved here**, including its very own existence. Once the player decides to leave the **Death City Mayfil** for the Divine Tree, it is **impossible to return to other parts of Endiness from thereafter**.

### Walkthrough

- The Divine Tree is a more or less **completely linear dungeon**, so the player simply has to **work the way upward**.
- In the **second screen**, there is a **well hidden Phoenix Plume** in a niche on the left side.
- Right after entering the **first upward stream**, the player can pick up the **Armor of the White-Silver Dragon** by interacting with the small shining gem on the floor. There also is a **Healing Spot** when interacting with the water, although the **player is fully healed after exiting the energy stream**.
- After the **Saving Spot**, the player has to fight the **Caterpillar mini-boss**. After defeating it, the player can enter the **upward stream** in the next screen to proceed to the **Moon That Never Sets**, the final location of the game.

### Treasure

- **White Silver Dragon's Armor**

The Divine Tree has a **minor boss encounter** with a **insectoid species which originated from an unhatched fruit left over**. The boss fight has **three phases**, after each of which the **form and abilities of the boss change**.

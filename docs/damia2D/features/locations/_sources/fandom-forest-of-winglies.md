The Forest of Winglies is a Wingly city in Mille Seseau and **Meru's hometown**. The Winglies have built a self-sufficient society there, **living a peaceful existence apart from humans**. **Legends and stories of "Fairies" in the northern part of the Evergreen Forest** exist all around Mille Seseau, likely referring to the Winglies living in the forest.

## Story

### Chapter 3: Fate and Soul

#### First Visit

During the courses of Chapter 3, when Dart and his companions go to Deningrad, Meru seperates herself temporarily from the group, saying that she wants to visit her parents. She enters the Forest of Winglies, where she is immediately attacked by a **fireball spell** by the guardian, who actually is her old acquaintance **Guaraha** and in shock to see her back. Although Guaraha is happy to see her, he warns her that the other inhabitants of the forest might be less forgiving for **breaching the taboo on leaving the forest**.

Meru finds herself indeed not welcome at all, with many Winglies and particularly the **Bardel Brothers** shaming her for leaving the forest. The Ancestor **Blano** intervenes, but repeats the custom that it was not allowed for Meru to leave.

> "Leaving the forest is an **unforgivable mortal sin**. Didn't you know that when you left for the world of Humans? As well as that, **you could never come back here**. There are people who hate and detest Humans just because they are Humans, but their number is few. We just want to protect our life. That's all. **Humans have always been afraid of the magic power of Winglies. The fear eventually turns itself into a need to tyrannize us, and take some action. Either to subjugate or kill Winglies.** That's why we need our own world, like this forest, where Humans cannot enter. Go back to the other world. Your existence angers the others." — Ancestor Blano

> _The Bardel Brothers sending Meru away._

Although **Meru's mother and father** appear at the scene, they do not voice any disagreements, and Meru is sent away.

As Meru wants to leave the forest again, Guaraha **snatches her from the air** and talks to her one more time. Guaraha tells Meru that everyone is feeling tense due to the **Divine Dragon coming back to life and the seal binding it dissolving**. It also appears to be the reason why **animals in the forest act savage**, and on Meru's response that the Winglies should have the **Dragon Buster and Dragon Block Staff**, Guaraha says that the **Dragon Buster**, that was locked together with the Dragon Block Staff **in a place only the Ancestor can open, was lost**. Lastly, Guaraha begs Meru not to leave again and vows to protect her, but Meru goes off again, saying that her friends are waiting for her.

#### Second Visit

> _Meru opens the magical gate towards the Forest of Winglies._

After the awakening of the Divine Dragon, Dart and his group consult with Queen Theresa and the Sacred Sisters of Mille Seseau on what to do, and on the recommendation of Rose, go on to search for the Dragon Block Staff. In the Evergreen Forest, Meru reveals to the group that she is a Wingly herself, and opens up the portal into the Forest of Winglies.

In the Forest, they encounter three Winglies who discuss that according to the ancestor, **cooperation with other species will be necessary to prevent their extinction** and to fight against the Divine Dragon. Upon seeing Meru, Dart and the others, one Wingly immediately flies off to inform the ancestor, while the other two stay on guard. The ancestor declares he wants to meet with the Humans, and Dart and his group walk through the Wingly community without resistance, until the **Older Bardel Brother** confronts them, hurling a fireball at them. Dart transforms into the **Red-Eyed Dragoon** and counters the spell; the Bardel brother, overcome by the sight of his magic resisted so easily, **falls to his knees and loses the will to fight**. The **Younger Bardel** finds him later, knocks him down to the ground and **vows revenge on the humans for killing his little sister, who also left the forest in the past**.

In the Forest, the group also meets with Meru's parents, who after a short conversation express some sympathy for Dart and Meru's friends. Upon meeting Guaraha, **who is on probation due to letting Meru pass through**, it is revealed that **Guaraha is actually her fiancée**. Meru affirms that she will be alright, together with Dart and her friends, and gives a slight poke at the obedient attitude of Guaraha before leaving.

> _Dart and his group meet Ancestor Blano._

Eventually, Dart and his friends meet Ancestor Blano, who welcomes them and tells them about the need to cooperate in order to survive against the Divine Dragon.

> "Welcome. **Humans and Giganto**. I am Blano the Ancestor of the forest. [...] The Divine Dragon came back to life in the Mountain of the Mortal Dragon, and there are Dragoons that have come to us. It seems that the times won't leave us alone. The **messenger from the Crystal Palace**, and the people who were fated to come here.... Our opponent is so enormous. We have to combine each of our strengths or none of us will survive. Neither the Crystal Palace nor our forest. [...] Let us put the power of the Dragoons and our wisdom together. [...] **We don't have the Dragon Buster now. So the Dragon Block Staff is our last resort.** We no longer have the power to master that, however, if you, who rule the Dragons can use it, we can fight against the Divine Dragon." — Ancestor Blano

> _Dart and his group being teleported to the Forbidden Land from the top of the Forest._

The ancestor brings them as close as he can to the Dragon Block Staff; they go to the top of the Forest of Winglies, where **a complex of ramps and towers stands below the Moon That Never Sets**. He directs Dart and his group to the **Forbidden Land**, telling them a bit about its history.

> "The Dragon Block Staff is sealed in the Forbidden Land over there. It is the place where our hideous memory sleeps. **It used to be, until 11,000 years ago, a palace for us Winglies and it was the place where eternal prosperity was promised. It was called the royal capital Kadessa. Now it is a mere ruin without a name.**" — Ancestor Blano

The Ancestor opens a **twisting tunnel of shifting metallic rings that leapfrog their way across the sky**, carrying the group all the way to the ruins of the ancient Wingly capital showing both the ruination of the war and the grand architecture of the civilization that preceded it.

WARNING: Spoilers follow from here.

After Dart and his group retrieve the Dragon Block Staff from the Forbidden Land, the **Divine Dragon finally sets out to destroy Deningrad**. Upon the return of Dart and his companions, the ancestor summons his powers to teleport Dart and his group there. However, **he fails in doing so, as his magical power alone was insufficient**. However, with the support of **Guaraha and Meru's parents, who come to the aid, he succeeds in sending them to Deningrad**.

> "We believe in Meru!! And her friends who Meru believes in too!!" — Meru's Father

## Gameplay

### Walkthrough

#### First Visit

- On the first visit, there is not much the player can do; after talking to Guaraha and using the teleporter to get into the main area the scenes where Meru is sent away will play.
- Meru will then speak to Guaraha and leave the forest again.

#### Second Visit

- After speaking to the Winglies at the entry and going to the main area, the player must speak to the Wingly at the end next to the **Saving Spot** to be transported upwards. This allows the player to enter the main building.
- In the main building, the player can speak to Meru's parents for an optional scene in the northwestern room, otherwise it is possible to just proceed through the area towards the northeast where there are two teleporters.
- Using the left teleporter leads to the shops, and when using the left exit from the shop, it will lead to Guaraha's room where a Stardust can be found. On the right side of the shop room, **a statue of the Archangel can be found, and Meru will tell a brief story about it**.
- Using the right teleporter in the main building leads to the area where the second Stardust is found. In the pink-glowing room above, the confrontation with the Older Bardel takes place (the room can also be used as a **Healing Spot**).
- Exiting the pink room will lead outside, where another Wingly offers to take the player up to the ancestor. After the conversation with the ancestor, on the top of the Forest of Winglies, the player has to talk to the ancestor to be teleported to the Forbidden Land.

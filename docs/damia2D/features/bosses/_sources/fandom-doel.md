## Story

### Before the events of the game

> Main article: Serdian War

**Twenty years ago**, **then-Prince Doel murdered his brother King Carlo** in an attempt to seize the power in the country. However, as the **rightful heir to the throne of Serdio, then-6-year old Albert was crowned the next king**. Doel, refusing to accept this, **seceded and founded Imperial Sandora in the South**, has since then has been **at war with the Kingdom of Basil in the north**, ruled by Albert.

Until recently the war had been **evenly matched, and a truce was declared**. However, when **Sandora gained the power of a Dragon, Feyrbrand**, the balance of power was tipped in their favor, and the truce was broken, leading to a re-ignition of the war.

### Chapter 1: The Serdian War

> _Doel and Lloyd — Doel talking to Lloyd._

> WARNING: Section contains spoilers.

Doel **ordered the capture of Shana on request of his advisor Lloyd**. She was found in Seles ; the **Great Commander** as well as **Fruegel** lead the attack, with the latter ordering the **town to be burned to the ground**.

As the war progresses, Sandora attacks both **Hoax** and the **Seventh Fort in the Marshlands**. While Dart and his group set off to confront the Dragon at the **Nest of Dragon**, he orders an all-out attack with almost the entire remaining Sandoran Knighthood to **occupy the capital of Basil, Bale**, leading to the practical victory in the war despite losing the Dragon. In Kazas, some Sandoran Soldiers praise their Emperor for his **boldness and calculating warfare**.

In exchange for the life of the residents of Basil, **King Albert offers himself as a hostage and is imprisoned in Hellena**.

This sets the events at the end of Disc 1 in motion : After Albert is freed from Hellena, Dart, Rose, Shana, Haschel and their **new companion Albert** decide to go to the **Black Castle in Kazas** to get answers from Doel and to **end the war for good**. With the help of **Popo from the New Serdio Party**, a **civil movement in Kazas**, they are able to gain access to the Black Castle.

After the **battle with Kongol**, they are able to meet the Emperor face to face. King Albert asks Doel **why he killed his father**, to which Doel responds he wanted to **save Serdio of an incapable ruler**. When Dart asks why he ordered the kidnap of Shana in Seles, he told him that this was the **agenda of Lloyd and Emperor Diaz**. Rose was in disbelief ; she said that this was impossible, as **Diaz died during the Dragon Campaign 11,000 years before the end of the game**. Emperor Doel **refused to elaborate** ; he challenged them to fight him for the answers they sought.

> **"Find the girl by the time the Moon That Never Sets waxes..." All is ordered by the reborn Emperor Diaz. Lloyd is a mere pawn. Along with the Dragoons...**
> — Doel

During the battle, Doel **reveals himself as the Dragoon of the Violet Dragon**. After his defeat, as observed by Albert, **Doel returns to his old self**, commending his nephew for becoming strong and to seek not the past, but the future, and to go and find out about the world. He **directs his nephew to pursue Lloyd** and find out about everything. Albert salutes to his dying uncle, as he vanishes.

### Chapter 4: Moon & Fate

> WARNING: Section contains spoilers.

When Dart and his group face **trials of courage in the Magical City Aglis**, Emperor Doel appears in the **trial of Kongol**, accusing his former loyal warrior for siding with his enemies and abandoning his vision for unifying Endiness. Kongol, however, stays courageous, **takes Doel's sword and refuses to apologize or back down** and passes the test.

> **Emperor Doel, Kongol got new feeling. Kongol found new friends. Intention of Emperor Doel is in Kongol. Kongol won't avoid Emperor Doel.**
> — Kongol

In the **Moon That Never Sets**, Dart and his group are set before **individual trials**. Upon seeing the rescue of the young Kongol by Emperor Doel, **Albert sets out to find out more about his uncles motifs**. He finds him in the **castle of Bale 20 years ago**, and as he meets with his uncle in the throne room, he presses Doel on **why he killed his father**.

Doel states that he did it to **"save Serdio from an incompetent leader named Carlo"**, to which Albert replies that the king, however, was loved by his people and his vassals. Doel, again, points out **overtaxation, disorder and corrupt ministers under the reign of Carlo**, and challenges Albert to a fight.

As they fight, Doel emphasizes the **need for strong leadership and power**, while Albert tells Doel that the **people do not desire power, but peace**. Albert emerges victorious from the duel, telling Doel that he does not only believe in his power, but **has his friends at his side**.

> **I'm different from you and I am not alone. My friends have always been at my side during the many occasions when I was dispirited. I've overcome difficulties. When I was young, my father would often say that he didn't quite have the stature to be King. Without Doel, he couldn't do anything. He also said that it is you who was most likely to take the throne. My father trusted in you. If you had gotten together and governed the country, this tragedy would never have happened.**
> — Albert

## Personality

In a strong contrast to his nephew Albert and possibly to his late brother Carlo, Doel puts a **strong emphasis on individual power and a strong leadership**.

He once was a **visionary with a strong conviction that all species originating from the Divine Tree are equal**. This vision of him was **one of the main incentives for Kongol, the last survivor of the Gigantos, for joining him**.

20 years before the event of the game, Doel's **dissatisfaction with the seemingly weak and careless rule of his brother Carlo** led to him killing his brother and trying to seize the throne himself.

As the Serdian War was re-ignited due to Sandora receiving the power of Dragoon Spirits and the Dragon Feyrbrand, **Doel started to become more aggressive again and turned into a wrathful, merciless ruler**, as the Great Commander of Sandora tells Dart and his group.

> **His Majesty Doel changed after a man called Lloyd showed up. He must have instilled something into Emperor Doel, a demonic word that ignites his desire to vanquish....**
> — Great Commander of Sandora

During this period of the Serdian War, Doel indeed appears to have become very cold-blooded, **using hell-on-earth-places such as Hellena Prison for his rule**, showing indifference towards the losses of the Dragon and Hellena and to the well-being of his people, as a **notable part of Kazas lives in poverty at an underground fort**, while he orders elaborate magical research to be conducted in the Black Castle. It is unsure whether Fruegel only tells this to Albert to taunt the king, but Fruegel tells Albert that **Doel intends to kill the people of occupied Bale despite of Albert offering himself as a hostage** in exchange for their life.

> _Faza — A statue of Empress Karina, the late wife of Emperor Doel._

Shortly before confronting Emperor Doel, **Father Faza** tells Dart and his group about the more humane sides of Doel, when they see a **large statue of his late wife, Empress Karina, in the Black Castle**.

> **The long war decays people's hearts. It must have happened to you too. The bloodstained armor tells all. However, His Majesty Doel is also experiencing the fatigue of war. This statue of a goddess was created in memory of the late wife of Emperor Doel, Empress Karina. It shows the frailty and fondness of Emperor Doel. He, however, continues the war for his own ambitions, with increasing scenes of carnage. It shows his strength and cruelness. Strength and frailty. Fondness and Cruelness. You must have them inside you. Because the human heart can envelop them all. Face up to your true self. Then you will become as strong as Emperor Doel.**
> — Father Faza

After his defeat, when the Emperor returns to his old self in his final moments, Doel praises his nephew for becoming strong and directs him to **look forward and find his answers for everything in Tiberoa**.

> **You have become... strong. What you should know is not the past, but the future. Pursue... Lloyd. He is in the Western country of... Tiberoa. He knows everything. Go, and find out...about the world, about...everything.**
> — Doel to Albert

## Gameplay

### Battle in the Black Castle

The battle in the Black Castle has **two phases**. During the first phase, he fights in his regular, **human form, wielding two swords**. This phase is considered relatively easy due to him having only a limited number of attacks. The battle gets more serious as after defeating his human form, he **turns into the Dragoon of the Violet Dragon**, giving him a substantial boost in power and abilities.

#### Normal Form (Emperor Doel, wields **two swords**)

- **Double Sword** : Doel **slashes one party member two times**, then **lifts them off the ground with his blades and slams them on the ground**.
- **Scream Bullet** : Doel **stabs his two swords on the ground**, then he and the swords start glowing yellow and start spinning around him and then he **throws both of his swords at one party member**.

#### Dragoon Form (Doel)

- **Charge Burst** : Doel shoots **two electric waves from his sword**, then raises his sword and **charges electrical energy to it and shoots it out at a party member**.
- **Barrier** : Doel creates an **electric barrier** that makes him **immune to all damage**, **lasting for three turns** _(vs wiki LoD "4 turns" — divergence)_.
- **Judgement Storm** : Doel **throws one of his swords up in the air over the party's head and charges an electrical storm**, then he **slashes each party member**, then he **flies up and grabs his sword where he absorbs all of the storms energy into the blade and sends the energy down towards the party with a swoop of his blade**, the energy descends down on the party and **explodes in an electrical explosion**.
- **Random Thunder** : Doel **spins around and releases a random max-level Spark Net or Thunderbolt** that deals major damage. He uses this often **while his Barrier is active**.

The battle with Doel begins **right after the battle with Kongol**, so it is advised to have a **reserve of healing items**. In his Dragoon form, he **casts Barrier after an unchanging amount of time, rather than an amount of damage or randomly** _(vs wiki LoD "HP <50% trigger" — divergence)_, so any player that can deal enough damage fast enough can **theoretically defeat Doel before he can raise the barrier**. It is recommended to heal with items or Guard when the barrier is up.

If the player knows that this downtime will happen, they might find **Therapy Ring or Spirit Ring** useful. A lot of boss battles later on will punish being in Dragoon form, and this one does too, albeit in a passive sort of way. Dragoon form lacks Guard or item use ; so being in Dragoon form when Doel raises the Barrier is **wasteful**. **Higher Dragoon level make the overall fight easier**, though, and Dragoon Magic can deal a lot of damage to him.

## Trivia

- **Doel is the only royal person of the game whose spouse, Empress Karina, is revealed by her name.** In contrast, his **brother Carlo's wife (the mother of Albert) is never revealed**, the wife of **King Zior of Tiberoa is unknown**, and it is **never exactly revealed whether Queen Theresa of Mille Seseau was actually married and had own biological children**, despite the events of the game surrounding the folklore about **Princess Louvia** hint at it.

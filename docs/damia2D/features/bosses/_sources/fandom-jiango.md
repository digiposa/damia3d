**Jiango is a beast kept in the Hellena Prison dungeon that the guards and wardens of the prison feed prisoners to**. **Dart and his company fight him after being maneuvered by the prison guards into standing on a trap-door**.

During **Lavitz's break-out of Hellena Prison at the first visit**, a conversation between escaping knights of Basil and Hellena Wardens hints at Jiango:

> **Hellena Warden**: "You really believe you can live comfortably in the jail? The head warden, **Fruegel will feed you to the monster!**"
>
> **Knight of Basil**: "Feed us to the monster!? I won't let you humiliate us anymore!"
>
> **Hellena Warden**: "Oh, feisty feisty. It's the **worst kind of POW¹ [sic], but the best kind of feed**."

## Strategy

Jiango is a **very powerful boss, with 1280 health and powerful Earth Element attacks**, particularly his "**Rock Slide**" attack, where **rocks fall the cave ceiling when he jumps up and down**. This attacks **hits all three fighting characters and can do over 100 damage on the characters**. His **breath has about a 50% chance to cause Confusion on an ally** and is very dangerous as well, it is advised to have **Mind Purifiers ready for the battle**.

**Magical attacks, particularly of the Wind element are very effective** in this fight, particularly **Wind attack items**. **Lavitz** might also inflict decent damage if he has his **third spell Gaspless** available at the time. In order to protect against the "Rock Slide" attack, **Rose Storm, Lavitz' second Dragoon spell, is also greatly recommended**.

The **Sachet found in his chamber will put Jiango to sleep and immobilize him for a while**, and it is recommended to **stick to magical attacks while he is asleep** to use this to the best advantage. However, **Sachets are also a powerful tool to defeat Unique Monsters**, so if the player is confident, it might be advisable to **keep it and fight Jiango fully awake**.

## Trivia

- **Jiango's design and motif are similar to that of the Rancor beast in the movie, Star Wars Episode VI: Return of the Jedi**. They are both **giant monsters kept in a dungeon that is only accessible through a trap door**, who **feed upon prisoners**, and are owned by **unscrupulous and corpulent antagonists, Fruegel and Jabba the Hutt respectively**.
- At the beginning of the battle, **for some unknown reason, Dart knows both Jiango's name and the fact that he can be defeated easier when he is put asleep**.

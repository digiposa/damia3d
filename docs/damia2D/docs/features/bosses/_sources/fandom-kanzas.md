## Story

### Chapter 2: Platinum Shadow

When **Rose is stranded together with Dart in Lidiera**, she remembers the **final battle of the Dragon Campaign**. In her memories, **Kanzas fought a Super Virage**. To **destroy it, he unleashed a mighty self-destruction attack**.

> _Dart and Rose meet Kanzas in his tower._

### Chapter 4: Moon & Fate

When **Dart, Rose and the others return to Vellweb to free the souls of the four Dragoons that died in the Dragon Campaign**, it is revealed that **Kanzas greatly enjoyed fighting and killing and kept clay dolls of the people he killed**. As **Rose criticizes the bad taste of keeping these clay dolls, Kanzas feels provoked and challenges the group to a fight**.

After being defeated by Dart, Rose and their group, **Kanzas expresses some regret to not have been able to have the "nobleness" of Shirley, and was hoping to find it even after death**. It is also revealed by Rose that **it was Shirley who convinced Kanzas to join in the fight for freedom**.

## Trivia

- According to **The Legend of Dragoon Official Guidebook, Kanzas was 165cm (5'5") tall and 45 years old at the time of the Dragon Campaign**.
- Within the game, **there is contradicting evidence on who of the original Dragoons fought with the Super Virage**. The **cutscene of Rose remembering the Dragon Campaign during chapter 2 unambiguously shows Kanzas destroying the Super Virage by self-destructing**. However, within the game, **upon encountering the Scarred Super Virage in the Forbidden Land, Rose figures that this "must be the Virage that killed Belzac"**. **Belzac himself, upon being confronted in Vellweb, remembers that he "[...] took the attack of Super Virage, with [his] own body...to be the shield for Shirley"**. In the cutscene, however, **Belzac is never seen to fight with the Super Virage, instead dying from being pierced by the claw from a normal Virage while stopping a collapsing roof from crushing him and Shirley**. This **contradiction is never resolved within the game**.

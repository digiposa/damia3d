The **Archangel is a deity worshipped by the Winglies in the Forest of Winglies**, shown as an **angel with four feathered wings and six arms**. In his arms, he wields a **book, a staff, a shield, a sword, a black flower, and a horn**.

## Story

### Chapter 3: Fate & Soul

Upon their **second visit to the Forest of Winglies**, Dart, Meru and their friends are welcomed into the forest, as **Ancestor Blano** decides on the necessity of the species to cooperate in order to fight the Divine Dragon. In the room where the Wingly shops are located, Meru introduces Dart to a **statue of the Archangel**, explaining that he is **worshipped as a guardian god**.

> "It's the Archangel! It's the guardian god of the Winglies! The Ancestor told me. [...] I dunno. I've never been protected. Maybe, I just don't know though. Okay! End of tour!"
> — Meru

### Chapter 4: Moon & Fate

> WARNING: Section contains spoilers.

In the **Moon That Never Sets**, the group continues through the floating lands beyond where they have been separated, and notice a **giant floating castle in the skies, reminiscent of the floating Wingly cities from the ancient times**. As they seek to enter the castle, the **bridge disappears** and **Meru is teleported into the palace alone**. Before her stands the Archangel, who tells her that her ways are wrong and that she should not side with the Humans, and that **they are a race deserving of being ruled upon or else they might just go extinct**.

Meru fights with the Archangel and is tested in her will and resolve, as he confronts her with claims about Wingly superiority. **Eventually, with her unshakable love for her friends and fellow humans, Meru makes the vision of the Archangel disappear**.

In the battle, the Archangel explains to Meru that **Wingly dominance over the other species is necessary for the survival of all**, and claims that **of the 107 species created from fruits of the Divine Tree, over half are extinct, a trend ended by the Winglies**. However Meru remarks that **many of those were exterminated by the Winglies themselves**, to which the Archangel replies that they **were meant to become extinct from the beginning**. Eventually, Meru is victorious and casts the Archangel away.

> "You are not real! You are an invented god so they can justify themselves, Archangel. [...] I love Humans! I adore Dart, Shana and the others! This is real!! Get of out my sight!! I love Humans."
> — Meru to the Archangel

### Strategy

⚠️ **As with all the Moon solo combats, the Dragoon form cannot be used.**

The Archangel has **extremely high Magic Defense** and has one all-out attack that **reduces Meru's HP to 1**. This can be **quickly countered by using a healing fog or similar item**. In certain instances, however, **the Archangel will heal Meru sometimes in battle**.

To win the Battle, **Meru simply has to fight her way through**, and **in contrast to other battles in the Moon That Never Sets, it is not necessary to choose between a correct and wrong answer**.

### Attacks (fandom canon names)

The Archangel uses the following attacks:

- **Manju Dagger**: Regular physical attack
- **Wingly Army**: Reduces target HP to 1
- **Heaven Spell**: Light or Thunder magic attack
- **Flower Gift**: Heals **1350 HP**
- **Healing Gift**: Heals Meru's HP to maximum

## Trivia

- ⚠️ **Archangel's special summoning attack is a possible reference to the Buddhist legend of Mara**, a demon who headed a fiend army from atop a monstrous elephant named **Girimekhala** in an attempt to prevent **Gautama** from meditation and enlightenment.
- ⚠️ **In Ulara, the other living Wingly city shown in the game, no traces of the Archangel can be found**, so it is open whether he is **actually worshipped by all Winglies or only by those in the Forest of Winglies**.
- ⚠️ **It is unknown whether the Archangel is a Wingly himself or not**. Melbu Frahma, the ancient dictator of the Winglies, also has **little similarity to the majority of "regular" Winglies**.

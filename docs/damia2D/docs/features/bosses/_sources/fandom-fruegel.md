Fruegel is the **head warden of Hellena Prison and a commanding officer of Imperial Sandora**. He is **feared even by his own people for his immense size and extremely brutal and sadistic personality**. He has **major problems controlling his anger**, shown on many occasions where he **kills his own men or threatens them**.

## Story

Fruegel is among the commanders given the order from **Emperor Doel** to obtain the girl named Shana from the small village Seles in the Kingdom of Basil. Brutish Fruegel is not content with only kidnapping the girl, **giving orders to mercilessly destroy the village as well**. This is revealed by Fruegel in Hellena during the first fight, and confirmed later in the game by the **Great Commander of Sandora in the Black Castle**, who **despised the unnecessary killing**.

> "That was done by the brutal unit loyal to Fruegel. I wouldn't let them kill unnecessarily if I were there..." — The Great Commander of Sandora

## First Battle

The first encounter with Fruegel is right after rescuing Shana and attempting to escape Hellena Prison. He has several attacks:

- **Attack** : Fruegel hits a party member with his club.
- **Body Slam** : Fruegel lifts a party member up, and then slams them on the ground.
- **Rock Throw** : Fruegel throws a large rock at a party member, this attack deals most damage.
- **Power up** — If hit with magic, **Atk & Def x1.5**, lasting **until the end of his 3rd turn**.

Fruegel will be accompanied to two Hellena Wardens at the start of battle, and they will have to be dealt with **before before anything can be done to Fruegel**. Once the Wardens are gone, Fruegel will **summon two new enemies, the Senior Wardens**. There are a couple of things to note at this point. The **Senior Wardens will remain after Fruegel is defeated** and they will **each use Gushing Magma once they themselves are defeated**. Finally, Fruegel will use a Power Up if you attempt to attack with magic items.

### Minions

#### Hellena Warden

> Main article: Hellena Warden

These Hellena Wardens have slightly higher HP but are the same in all other regards. They can be taken out in a few turns. It must be noted that **before both are killed, Fruegel can't be attacked**. Once both are taken care of, Fruegel will summon two Senior Wardens to keep fighting beside him.

#### Senior Warden

> Main article: Senior Warden

Senior Wardens first appear when fighting against Fruegel the first time in Hellena after the **two Hellena Warden Fruegel called "wimps"** were defeated. They will attack your allies with **double voulge**. After their defeat, they will cry "**Huh, I may as well take you to hell with me!**" and **throws a Gushing Magma on you**. A few turns after the first Senior is defeated, the second will **power up and will only take half its normal damage**. To counteract this, it is suggest to attack both wardens at the same time so that once the first is defeated, the second can be killed immediately after. **Under speed-running conditions, it is possible to kill Fruegel before he summons them.**

### Strategy

The Hellena Wardens must be taken out first in this battle, and **magical attack items, particularly when used with Shana (who has a significantly better magical attack than Lavitz and Dart)** can accelerate this. Afterwards, it is possible to either target Fruegel or the Senior Wardens first, although the player should decide on one strategy at the beginning. A possible strategy for a quick victory is to target Fruegel exclusively with magical attack items. He will use a Power Up because of this, but even so, **magical damage will reduce his HP quickly. He has lower magic defense and the whole party -possibly with the exception of Lavitz due to his poor magical attack- will do more damage by using Burn Out or Spark Net over Additions**. After he's defeated, the same method can be used on his minions if magical attack items are left. If done quick enough, there should be no need to heal in this fight, but you find your self low on health, Guard or heal until healthy before defeating the final Wardens or after defeating one Warden, as they will use a Gushing Magma before dying.

## Second Battle

The second time you encounter Fruegel is **after returning to Hellena Prison to rescue King Albert**. During this encounter he'll fight alongside his **pets, Rodriguez and Guftas**. Fruegel roughly has the same attack pattern as before, albeit **much stronger** and will also **issue unique commands to his pets as long as they're still in battle**.

Fruegel can use a variety of attacks including:

- **Attack** : Fruegel hits a party member with his club.
- **Throat Chop** : Fruegel chops a party member a few times then knocks them to the ground.
- **Rock Throw** : Fruegel throws a large rock at a party member.
- **Power Up** : Fruegel will use this after being hit with magic, increasing his offenses and defenses by 50%.
- **Command** : Fruegel will order Guftas or Rodriguez to do a unique attack.

**Rodriguez is Fruegel's pet bird.**

- **Feather Shot** : Rodriguez throws sharp feathers from its wings to harm a party member.
- **Aerial Attack** : When called by his master to attack, he uses an aerial drop attack on one party member, which causes considerable damage.

#### Guftas

**Guftas is Fruegel's pet dog. He wears a helmet with a spike on top of it.**

- **Bite** : Guftas runs up to a party member, puts his front legs on their shoulders, and bites to harm a party member.
- **Howl** : When called by his master, he unleashes a loud howl to **deal confusion to a party member**.

### Strategy

This fight can be a lot easier than his first encounter if you are prepared. If you saved the **Total Vanishing items you could obtain from the Prairie and Limestone Cave**, you can use them on the pets to deal with them instantly. **The game does not recognize Guftas and Rodriguez as boss monsters and therefore they are not immune to Total Vanishing**. **Rose's Demon's Gate will have the same effect and can be done in one turn if you've managed to learn it by this point** (which is unlikely, but possible in theory). Alternatively, and arguably a less optimal route, you could ignore Guftas and Rodriguez altogether and use **Spinning Gale on Fruegel with all three members**. Once he's defeated, his pets will flee. It's advised to deal with the pets first if you're able to quickly defeat them because with all three of them alive, they attack much more frequently and will easily whittle down your team. Whether you deal with the pets or not, **using Spinning Gales on Fruegel with all party members will deal significant damage and end the fight soon**.

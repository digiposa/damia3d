There are **53 obtainable pieces of armor** in The Legend of Dragoon. The following tables below list all types of armor obtainable in the game for each playable character with their bonus to respective attributes.

A handful of armor items drop from minor enemies and may be farmed over and over. Their percentages are listed in the Location column. There are also one-time boss drops, and not all are guaranteed. Those percentages are also listed in the Locations column.

Default sort for the tables is by the order of availability in the game.

### Guidance on Helms/Headwear

> Note: This section is subjective and may not apply to each player individually.

As a general rule, Headwear is supposed to improve magical attack, so instead of focusing on the improvement of defense, better headwear should mainly be assigned to characters meant to use either **Dragoon Magic or Magical Attack Items**. On top of this, the **Sallet** and the **Tiara** are useful against enemies that are more difficult to hit, particularly Unique Monsters. The **Armet** has the best Magical Defense of all "regular" helms for male characters and as such might be a better choice for Lavitz/Albert than other headwear such as the Knight Helm.

The **Legend Casque** is considered among the best items in the entire game, as enemies rely increasingly on strong magical attacks as the game progresses. Not only does it give an unparalleled boost for Magical Defense, but also gives an astonishing 50% Magical Evade, making any character equipped with it close to invulnerable against magical attacks. **It is considered a crucial item to defeat Faust**, however, the massive 10,000G it costs make it all but unattainable unless the player heavily grinds for Gold (e.g. by farming 00PARTS or the Red Bird).

Apart from this, the **Dragon Helm** is very useful for characters with low HP such as Rose and Meru and is **additive to the effects of the Physical Ring**, but it also offers a massive 50 Magical Attack. The **Magical Hat** is also very useful for characters that are supposed to use a lot of Dragoon Magic and improves Magical Defense as well. The **Phoenix Plume** is also useful, although Status Ailments become increasingly rare in the later game, however its Magical Attack and Magical Defense boost are noteworthy.

### Guidance on Armor

> Note: This section is subjective and may not apply to each player individually.

With the focus on improving Physical and Magical Defense, armor should be assigned in the way the respective character benefits the most of it. The priority of Physical and Magical defense should be considered carefully; for characters with weak base Magical Defense such as Lavitz/Albert and Kongol, it may be a better choice to give priority to Magical Defense to compensate for the characters' weaknesses.

Late in the game in Zenebatos or on the Moon, the player can obtain the **DG Armors**, individual pieces of armor for each character that both offer high Physical and Magical Defense (although not necessarily the highest possible for each character). There is speculation that these are the character's Dragoon Armor pieces (see Trivia) and they **nullify any elemental damage** dealt to the character with the respective element. However, depending on the task at hand, the DG Armors are not necessarily the best choice for every fight; other pieces of armor with beneficial side effects such as SP gain or preventing Status Ailments can be more useful in certain battles depending on the player's strategy.

A notable armor for female characters is the **Angel Robe**. While not offering any bonus to DF or M DF, it gives a chance to revive the character when knocked out in battle. **The probability to revive is estimated between 45-49%** and is **actually additive with Holy Ankh (also estimated at a 45-49% chance)**, giving an incredibly high chance of revival if used together. However, it is **rumored that sometimes after swapping disks, they both must be re-equipped or only one of the two will affect the revival chance** leaving Shana/Miranda or Meru with slightly less than a 50% revival chance.

The **Armor of Legend** is the physical equivalent of the Legend Casque and is obtainable to the same price, but is **considered vastly inferior**, as boss enemies increasingly use powerful magical attacks as the game progresses, and physical attacks by enemies deal mostly minor or moderate damage.

### Guidance on Boots

> Note: This section is subjective and may not apply to each player individually.

With the exception of Speed bonuses, boots mostly offer minor bonuses, and as such are generally less crucial for a character's performance in comparison to Helms/Headwear and Armor. The **Bandit's Shoes** and **Dancer's Shoes** are only found in one chest of the game respectively until the Divine Tree. The **Magical Greaves** are a good all-around item with a boost to both Speed and Physical and Magical Avoidance and are very recommendable to male characters, as no regular male boots give a boost on M-AV. For the same reasons as stated before for Helms/Headwear and Armor, **Magical Avoidance may be considered slightly superior to Physical Avoidance**, as enemies rely mostly on strong magical attacks later in the game.

## Trivia

The actual name of the "DG Armors" is only seen twice in the game, when the **Silver DG Armor is found in a chest on the Divine Tree** and the **Golden DG Armor is found in a chest in the Moon That Never Sets** (see the respective gallery). There, the names of the armors are written as **"White Silver Dragon Armor"** and **"Golden Dragon Armor"** instead of **"[...] Dragoon Armor"**, although there is speculation that these would be equivalent (as Miranda is the "Dragoon of the White-Silver Dragon" and Kongol is the "Dragoon of the Gold[en] Dragon").

There are **49 Accessories** in The Legend of Dragoon. They can be found in treasure chests, won in battle, and purchased in Shops. Accessories are used to enhance a characters stats or simply prevent abnormalities or even death.

All Accessories can be used on any character except for the **Dancer's Ring** and **Bandit's Ring**, which are equivalent in effect but for female and male characters only, respectively. The **Wargod Calling** and **Ultimate Wargod** cannot be equipped by Shana/Miranda, who have no Additions.

## Guidance on Accessories

> Note: This section is subjective and may not apply to each player individually.

### General

The choice of Accessories for a certain character can depend greatly on the player's strategy. As a general recommendation, Accessories should regularly be switched out depending on the task at hand and expected battles ahead. Although many Accessories sell for a good amount of money, the player may consider keeping them, as most accessories can have a niche use throughout the game and there are other methods of obtaining Gold (e.g. by farming Unique Monsters such as **00PARTS and the Red Bird**).

### Improving Stats

> Martel gives out numerous useful Accessories when trading in Stardust.

A number of Accessories improve a character's Attributes/Stats; of these, the **Attack Badge, Guard Badge, Giganto Ring, Magical Ring and Spiritual Ring** offer particularly good bonuses for Physical and/or Magical Attack and Defense. There are also useful items for improving other Stats, such as the **Bandit's/Dancer's Ring (Speed)**, which give a tactical advantage in battles since the character will have more turns, the **Elude/Sage/Spirit Cloak** which somewhat increase Avoidance Stats (although avoidance will still be luck dependant) and the **Wargod's Amulet**, which will significantly improve hit percentages and is **particularly useful to defeat Unique Monsters**. HP and MP can also be improved by the **Physical Ring (HP)** and the **Amulet (MP)**, both of which are obtained from Martel.

For improving a character's stats, the player can follow different strategies. It is possible to compensate for a character's weaknesses, e.g. by equipping Meru with the Giganto Ring or the Physical Ring, or giving the Spiritual Ring or the Bandit's Ring to Albert or Kongol. It is, however, also possible to follow different strategies, e.g. to give the Amulet to one's favorite Dragoon to use their Dragoon Magic more frequently, or to give Meru or Shana/Miranda the Dancer's Ring to give them enormous speed.

### Recovering Accessories

Some Accessories generate HP, SP or MP under certain conditions or at the beginning of each turn. The **Therapy Ring** is considered particularly useful, as it recovers 10% of the characters' HP each turn, and is available quite early in the game. The **Mage Ring** is also useful for characters that are supposed to use Dragoon Magic frequently.

⚠️ **It should be noted that Therapy Ring, Spirit Ring and Mage Ring do NOT work when a character is in the Dragoon form.**

> The Jewellery Shop in Fletz, where a Stardust can be found as well.

There are four Accessories that can be purchased in the Jewellery Shop at Fletz that recover either MP or SP when hit with either a physical or magical attack:

- **Platinum Collar** (MP/physical)
- **Sapphire Pin** (MP/magical)
- **Ruby Ring** (SP/magical)
- **Emerald Earring** (SP/physical)

Generally, characters are hit much more often with physical damage than with magical damage, and SP are usually easier to collect than MP, so the **Platinum Collar and the Emerald Earring can be considered superior** to the other two.

Another notable item is the **Holy Ankh**, which revives a fallen character with a probability **estimated at around 45-49%** at the moment the character is knocked out. This effect is cumulative with the effect of the **Angel Robe** (see Armor) and allows for an extremely high chance of revival for a character using both items simultaneously.

### Protective Accessories

A lot of Accessories available early in the game prevent a certain Status Ailment. For using these to good value, however, the player requires knowledge about which monsters and bosses can inflict which specific Status Ailment. The respective Accessories are:

- Active Ring (Dispiriting)
- Bravery Amulet (Fear)
- Destone Amulet (Petrification)
- Magical Ego Bell (Bewitchment)
- Panic Guard (Confusion)
- Poison Guard (Poison)
- Protector (Arm Block)
- **Rainbow Earring** (all Status Ailments)

⚠️ **It is disputable whether "Can't Combat" is an actual Status Ailment**, however it is possible to protect a character against this by using the **Talisman**. This is particularly useful against some bosses such as the **Complete Virage, Mappi, the Polter Armor or Kubila**.

It should be noted that certain Armor prevents Status Ailments or Can't Combat as well, making the use of some of the beforementioned Accessories obsolete.

> In Lohan, the merchant on the top left corner sells particularly expensive Accessories and Armor.

Particularly expensive Accessories are the three that half certain types of damage and can be purchased in Lohan:

- **Phantom Shield** (10,000G; halves all damage)
- **Dragon Shield** (5,000G; halves physical damage)
- **Angel Scarf** (5,000G; halves magical damage)

Their value relative to their cost is somewhat disputed, as the **Legend Casque** (see Armor; also 10,000G) is generally considered superior and **extremely valuable against Faust**, whereas neither the Phantom Shield nor the Angel Scarf offer the same protection. The Dragon Shield can also be considered subpar relative to its price, as boss enemies later in the game rely increasingly on strong magical attacks instead of physical attacks.

### Element Stones

> Laser attacks by Virages are of the Light Element, making the Silver Stone useful.

There are **seven stones corresponding to an Element**; they half all magical damage inflicted to a character who wears the stone of the respective element. Similar to Accessories preventing Status Ailments, they should be equipped when a boss fight is expected where the enemy uses strong magical spells of the respective element, or when frequently encountering enemies that use magic of a certain element (e.g. in Aglis).

A particularly notable example is **Zieg Feld on Disc 4, against whom the Red-Eye Stone (obtained from Firebird on Disc 1) should be used**, or the **Silver Stone (obtained from Shirley on Disc 1), which is useful against all Virages, as their laser attacks are of the Light Element**.

The other stones are the Darkness Stone (Darkness), Golden Stone (Earth), Jade Stone (Wind), Violet Stone (Thunder) and Blue Sea Stone (Water).

### Accessories supporting Additions

> The Ultimate Wargod can be purchased in Lohan or found in the treasure chest mini-game on the Phantom Ship.

There are two Accessories that make Additions complete (meaning the addition is done without needing to press a button), the **Wargod Calling** and the **Ultimate Wargod**.

- The **Wargod Calling** completes each addition but does **half damage, gives half SP and does not add to the use counter to increase the Addition Level**. It can be bought for 1,000 G at Lohan or Fletz.
- The **Ultimate Wargod** is much more expensive, but does **full damage, gives full SP and does add to the use counter** meaning it is completely successful. However, the 10,000 G required to buy it in Lohan or the extreme amount of either luck or patience at the Phantom Ship as well as it blocking the Accessory Spot for basically no inherent advantage apart from easier or more relaxed battles make it a somewhat unrewarding item.

### Other

The **Wargod's Sash** raises the SP obtained by 50% and is very useful for grinding SP and improving Dragoon Levels, or when the player wants to use Dragoon forms frequently. It is, however, not always the best choice for improving a character's SP gain when using a low-SP addition, as the **Spirit Ring** or the **Emerald Earring** can gain more SP on average.

⚠️ **The break-even point for this is when using an addition that earns 40 SP**, as multiplying these with the factor 1.5 will gain 60 SP, the same a character would get when equipped with the Spirit Ring (40 SP with the addition + 20 SP at the beginning of each round). The Wargod's Sash gains more SP with all additions that gain more than 40 SP under the condition that the player executes these perfectly.

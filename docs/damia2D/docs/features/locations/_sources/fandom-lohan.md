**Lohan** (**商業都市ロアン, Shōgyō Toshi Roan, "Commercial City Loan"**) is a **large commercial town in Serdio**. At the time of the **Serdian War, it is a politically neutral town, located close to the borders of both Basil and Sandora**. It is the **site of the Hero Competition**.

## Story

### Chapter 1: The Serdian War

#### A Cure for Shana

After **fighting Greham and Feyrbrand in Nest of Dragon**, Dart and his companions reach Lohan where they **take the unconscious Shana to Sanator, the famous physician residing in the town**. After Shana is examined by the doctor, he reveals that the **only hope for a fast cure might be the Dragoni Plant, a plant only rumored to exist in legends**.

Upon visiting the town, the group **meets Dabas again** and ask him about the **Dragoni Plant**. Although he doesn't have it at his store, he looks it up in his notebook and finds a note about it.

> "**I remember hearing about it somewhere. Hold on a sec. Well, in this secret notebook.... Yes! It's in here! It says, North of the poisoned forest. Past the troublesome plant. Observed Dragoni Plant in back of the Shrine!**"
> — Dabas

> A merchant jokingly trying to sell the heroes a bottle for **100,000,000G**.

Dart, Lavitz and Rose **remember a mutated plant at the location where Shana rested when they first entered the Nest of Dragon**, and **come up with the idea of sprinkling it with the pure water from the nest to purify it and go past it**. They leave Dabas' Antiques without buying anything, and go to the main road of the town, where a **merchant offers them a bottle for the water for 100,000,000G**. The group refuses, but the **merchant says the offer was a joke, and offers it for 3,000G instead**. Dart **keeps negotiating and is eventually, after a moment of silence, receiving the bottle for free**. The group **heads back to the Nest of Dragon to get the pure water and to make their way to the Shrine of Shirley**.

#### The Return to Lohan

> The White-Silver Dragoon Spirit recognizes Shana and heals her from the poisoning.

After obtaining the **Dragoon Spirit of the White-Silver Dragon at the Shrine of Shirley**, Dart, Lavitz and Rose **return to Sanator and the clinic where Shana lies**. Dart **intends to use the Dragoon Spirit together with his own to set the healing powers free**, but the **Spirit suddenly glows brightly, recognizing Shana as its rightful wielder, and unleashing the healing powers by its own**.

The **Sanator is deeply impressed by the miracle he witnesses and realizes that it is Dart and his group that defeated the Dragon**. Upon this, he **recommends Dart to participate in the Hero Competition, a tournament to determine the strongest fighter of Endiness and held in Lohan at that time**.

#### The Hero Competition

Main article: Hero Competition

After the **awakening of Shana**, the group goes to the **"Hero Competition" at the arena of Lohan**; Dart signs up to participate. Before the contest starts, Dart **meets his old friend Haschel who has also signed up**. After catching up on events since their journey together, the contest begins. **Dart and Haschel, in different brackets, work their way through the tournament**. Dart **fights four opponents, Gorgaga, Serfius, Danton and Atlow**. **Offscreen, Haschel also progresses far into the tournament, before a mysterious swordsman named Lloyd unexpectedly defeats him**. Dart **expected to fight Haschel, but Lloyd is his opponent in the finals instead**. He **dodges Dart's attacks with inhuman speed, and launches devastating attacks**. After a short fight, **Lloyd finishes off Dart and becomes the winner of the contest**. Dart, **for making it to the finals, places second; Haschel places third**. In the back room, Dart **admits his defeat**, to which **Lloyd commends his opponents with somewhat mysterious comments**.

> "**You haven't reached your limits. You will be stronger. You will have to be. You too. You'll become stronger.**"
> — Lloyd to Dart and Haschel

After Dart receives Shana's congratulations for the second place and introduces Haschel to his friends, he and Shana **take off some time together in the arena to enjoy some games**. After they finished, **Lavitz goes outside with Dart for a moment, giving him some candid advice about giving more priority to Shana and to re-think his quest for revenge with the Black Monster**.

> "**Dart. Revenge doesn't solve anything. I learned that from the death of Greham, the guy who betrayed my father, the guy who defected to the Sandora.... The guy I hated. The person you should be looking for is not from your past. Right? You should pay attention to the present. I'll accept a couple of drinks for this advice. When the war ends, let's have a drink in Bale.**"
> — Lavitz to Dart

When the group **re-unites and sets out to report to King Albert in Bale** about their successful quest to overcome the Dragon, a **dying soldier of Basil stumbles into the town and tells Lavitz that Bale has fallen to a surprise attack of Sandora and that King Albert has been kidnapped and taken to Hellena Prison**. Lavitz is **enraged and immediately wants to head to Hellena to liberate his king**, but is **held back by Dart**. **Haschel rushes in and delivers a blow to Lavitz, knocking him unconscious**.

As Lavitz **awakes at the clinic where Shana was**, Haschel **scolds him for losing his self-control**. Haschel **decides to join the group in their quest, and they set out together to liberate the king**.

## Gameplay

> The center of Lohan. Blue door: Inn. Left red door: Clinic. The passage to the bottom left leads to a green door to the Item shop and the upstairs red door to the Weapon shop. The passage to the bottom right leads to Dabas' shop.

### Walkthrough

#### A Cure for Shana

- After leaving the clinic, the player should **go to Dabas' Antiques towards the bottom right from the center of Lohan**, and **also get the Angel Robe from the hidden chest in front of the store**.
- After talking to Dabas, the player must **go down the stairs outside towards the main road**. There, **the merchant will offer the bottle**. The player should **keep negotiating until eventually, at the price of 100G, the option "..." appears**. Upon **selecting this option, the merchant will give out the bottle for free**. The player can then **head to the left and exit the town, or keep exploring the town and gather some Stardust**.

#### Hero Competition

- After Shana is cured, the player should **make their way to the right from the "bottle merchant" on the main road, leading towards the arena**. The **registration counter for the tournament is on the eastern side where Ginger is**. The player then has to **fight Gorgaga, Serfius, Danton and Atlow, where it actually is optional to win the fights, as Dart will progress even if he should lose the fights**. The **final battle against Lloyd is unwinnable and will end after a certain number of rounds**.
- When **Dart is alone with Shana**, the player can play some **mini-games**. If this is not desired, the player can simply **go to another section of the arena and return to Lavitz**. After the **cutscene between Lavitz and Dart**, the player must **head towards the entrance of the town**, where another cutscene plays. After **Haschel joins the group**, the player can then **exit the town and head to Hellena Prison**.

#### The Dragoon Spirit of the Gold Dragon

⚠️ WARNING: Section contains spoilers.

After **Kongol joins the group in Disc 2**, **Mr. Pelpee can be found in the bar in Donau and tells the group of a mysterious stone similar to those the group is already carrying**. Seeing this conversation is completely optional, however, and the **Dragoon Spirit of the Gold Dragon can simply be obtained by re-visiting Lohan**. Upon coming to the main road, **the merchant who sold the bottle will talk to the group and will offer to sell them an "elegant golden stone" for 100g**. But when the **stone glows in response to Kongol, revealing it as the Golden Dragoon Spirit**, the **merchant takes it back and offers it as a "Shining Miracle Stone" for 1,000g**. Unlike with the bottle, **he will not budge on this price**. If the player intends to **use Kongol in battle, it is recommended to pay the 1000g to buy it**.

### Shops

There are **several shops in Lohan, although there is a bit of stair climbing and running along rooftops required to get to all of them**. The **weapon shop in Lohan is notorious for offering some extremely expensive but valuable Armor and Accessories**. Some of the **Accessories can, but not with certainty, be obtained as drops from bosses later in the game**. All the **5,000 and 10,000 G Armor and Accessories, though, are unique to Lohan, with the exception of the Dragon Shield and the Ultimate Wargod**.

A **particularly recommendable item, should the player obtain the necessary Gold, is the Legend Casque**. It **makes a character virtually invulnerable against Magical attacks, which are the main threat by stronger bosses later the game**. The **Ultimate Wargod is only recommended if the player struggles to perform additions consistently**.

### Arena

Minigames are **available here at most points in the game after the Hero Competition**.

Tickets can be bought for **10G each, if you buy 10 tickets**. For **five at a time, they cost 12G each, and single tickets cost 15G**. The tickets can be used for prizes. At **10G per ticket, the prizes cost**:

- **Spirit Potion: 20 tickets: 200G**
- **Total Vanishing: 40 tickets: 400G**
- **Healing Rain: 60 tickets: 600G**

With the **list provided in the What's Wrong With The Scene? section, the observation minigame is a foolproof source of two tickets (three minus the one for playing) every minute, or even as little as 30 seconds once the player gets fast**. Battles in **Black Castle run about a minute and gain 24G or so**. **Disc 2 battles can three times that, or more**. **Travel time to a combat area would be a factor, but less so for large amounts of tickets**. In conclusion, **on Disc 1, the "What's Wrong With The Scene?" game gains as much as normal combat except XP; once the observation game can be completed in less than a minute, it accumulates tickets faster**. The **ratio changes again on Disc 2; for the rest of the game, combat will gain money so fast that the observation game cannot keep up**.

#### Obstacle Course

While the **other minigames may test skills players are not familiar with, the obstacle course is merely an extreme test of the movement skills that we all use to play video games, including dealing with outsized boundary boxes**. **Merely coming too close to the moving obstacles will knock Dart out**.

> Man at Obstacle Course: "**Reach the goal before the top left count hits 400. There will be something good waiting for you if you reach the goal quickly! Move with the direction key and duck with the X button to avoid obstacles!**"
> Shana: "**Didn't you hurt yourself?**"
> Dart: "**I'm fine, I have my armor**"

#### What's Wrong With The Scene?

The **easiest game, in that it gets a lot easier in a relatively short time**. The **key to it is that the starting room is always the same, so once you memorize that, the changes are more obvious**.

A **room is revealed; ten locations inside the room are furnished with items**. A **curtain is drawn, and three of the items are replaced**. A **bird appears, which the player can move over the locations one after the other**. **Pressing the action button selects that item as the player's guess; if the player guesses wrongly, the game ends**. If the player **selects all three correctly they win three tickets, for a net gain of two**.

#### Who is the Man

One of the **utterly identical green-clad men hides a pink bird under his green hat and the men proceed to run about, weaving in between each other in a confusing pattern**.

> Shana: "**I feel dizzy.**"
> Dart: "**It's my kind of game, it's like a battle.**"

#### Monster Conquest

**Throw balls to hit monkeys and dodge the balls they throw at you**. At the **end, no matter your score, a scripted sequence with three monkeys throwing a barrage of balls at Dart**.

> Shana: "**You were really into it.**"
> Dart: "**I was so close to avoiding the last attack.**"
> Shana: "**Nobody could do that.**"
> Dart: "**Yes I can. Normally I should be able to do it.**"
